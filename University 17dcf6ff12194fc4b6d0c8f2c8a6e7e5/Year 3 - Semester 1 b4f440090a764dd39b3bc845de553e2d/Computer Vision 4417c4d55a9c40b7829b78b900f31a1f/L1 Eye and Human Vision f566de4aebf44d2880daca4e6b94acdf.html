Is human vision a good model for computer vision?

Human eye

Evolved for survival

Function of the eye is to form an image on the retina (on fovea)

The lens is shaped, rather than moved

Image is transmitted via optic nerve

The brain must invert the image afterwards, exactly like a camera.

Sensors

Colours come in three different types, encoded in cones (10^7)

Grey-level is encoded through rods (10^8)

Cones come in three types:

  • S – short wavelength (blue)
  • M – medium wavelength (green)
  • L – long wavelength (red)

Insufficient bandwidth of optic nerve implies coding

Spectral responses

Sometimes need to compensate responses in camera, etc…

Neural processing

Most computing techniques for computer vision are the same as the methods used in the brain. e.g., using edges to form shapes and distinguish between different items
