  • Performance same as template matching, but faster
  • A line is points (x,y) gradient m intercept c; y=m‚àóx+cy=m*x+cy=m‚àóx+cÔªø
  • and is points (m,c) gradient -x intercept y; c=‚àíx‚àóm+yc=-x*m+yc=‚àíx‚àóm+yÔªø

The coordinates of the peak are the parameters of the line (principle of duality)

i.e., every point on the initial (left) image, corresponds to their own line on the other (right) image

Yellow point is yellow line; blue point is blue line‚Ä¶

The point in the middle of the right image that intersects everything is the line in the left image that crosses every point

Could use this method for other things, such as edge detection to detect the line that intersect every shape, *yada yada‚Ä¶*

accum=0
for all x,y                      #look at all points
	if edge(y,x)>threshold         #check significance

			c = -x*m+y                 #calculate c
			accum(m,c) PLUS 1          #vote in accumulator
m,c = argmax(accum)              #peak gives parameters
