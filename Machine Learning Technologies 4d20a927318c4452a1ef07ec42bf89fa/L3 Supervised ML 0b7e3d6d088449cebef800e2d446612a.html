Data Representation

Discrimination

If you want to discriminate two sets of data, you draw a line between them :)

Drawing the normal line to discriminatory lines can help you figure out how certain your prediction is; as well as a classification way of finding any outliers

The reason to not use a non-linear line to discriminate the data is because it may result in overfitting

What does discriminative learning do?

  • We develop a model that learns to discover patterns in the data that can be used to make predictions for new, un-labeled data points

Advantages & Disadvantages

Generative:

  • Generative models are useful for unsupervised machine learning tasks.
  • Generative models are computationally expensive compared to discriminative models.
  • Generative models are impacted by the presence of outliers more than discriminative models.

Discriminative:

  • Discriminative learns to separate data so it is much easier to categorise new data as it arrives
  • Discriminative models are computationally cheap compared to generative models.
  • Discriminative models are useful for supervised machine learning tasks.
  • Discriminative models have the advantage of being more robust to outliers, unlike the generative models.
